# Career Fit Quality Gates

Career fit content connects occupations to RIASEC, personality/work-style language, and FermatMind test actions. It must avoid diagnosis, destiny, and success claims.

## PASS Requirements

- RIASEC is the primary career-interest signal and traces to O*NET or validated interest evidence.
- Big Five, MBTI, and work-style language are supplementary and clearly bounded.
- Every Big Five bridge input traces to a backend-published, public-safe,
  locale-matched projection; working/draft revisions and generated packages are
  absent from reader inputs.
- Fit signals are linked to real work activities, not only occupational stereotypes.
- Mismatch cues are framed as work-structure friction, not personal defect.
- CTA text is localized, reader-facing, and not presented as occupational fact.
- No claim that a test can determine career success, income, employment, mental health, or personal worth.
- `zh-CN` and `en` CTA and fit language are market-specific.
- No occupation ranking, job-fit score, trait-to-career pSEO grid, or "best
  careers for this trait" output is present.
- No private answers, scores, percentiles, selector traces, attempt/report URLs,
  user identity, order, or payment state is present.

## Block-Specific Failures

- personality type used as proof of occupation suitability
- Big Five publication inferred from record/import/route existence instead of
  a verified published public projection
- working/draft personality revision used as reader authority
- Big Five-only occupation ranking or job-fit score
- private assessment or commerce data used in a public fit asset
- generic "good fit if you like helping people" copy without workflow evidence
- repeated test-promo sentence across occupations
- hidden diagnosis or guaranteed outcome language
- AI Impact or salary score imported as fit evidence

## Editorial QA Focus

Sample high-pressure roles, creative roles, service roles, analytic roles, and regulated roles. The fit narrative should change with work structure, not just with title.

For Big Five samples, also verify high/middle/low trait language is value-neutral,
the same trait does not mechanically produce the same career list, and missing
published authority fails closed without fallback copy.
