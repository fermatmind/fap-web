# Career Fit Writing Rules

Frame fit as signals to test, not conclusions. RIASEC is the primary career-interest lens; personality is supplementary.

Reader-facing copy must be localized, role-specific, concise, and explicitly bounded when evidence is partial. Do not leak evidence IDs, raw enums, source collection notes, or internal audit language.

Use Big Five to describe questions a reader can test about feedback, structure,
pace, collaboration, autonomy, or stress context. Do not write "best careers for
high/low trait", deterministic fit, objective occupation ranking, hiring advice,
or guaranteed success/income/placement language. High, middle, and low trait
language must remain value-neutral and must not imply ability or personal worth.
