# AI Exposure Rubric

Use this rubric for the AI impact block only. It converts occupation-level task evidence into a bounded reader-facing AI exposure score.

## What the Score Means

`score_1_to_10` is a normalized task exposure score. It estimates how much of the occupation's recurring task structure may be accelerated, assisted, or partially automated by current or near-term AI systems.

It does not mean:

- probability of unemployment
- probability that the occupation disappears
- personal income prediction
- hiring outlook
- credential value
- whether one specific worker will be replaced

## Required Evidence Chain

Every AI score must trace through:

1. PASS occupation identity boundary.
2. PASS work-activities or task evidence.
3. Task-level evidence from O*NET tasks, Detailed Work Activities, work activities, or equivalent official occupation profiles.
4. At least one external calibration source for AI/task exposure methodology.
5. FermatMind internal rubric scoring notes.
6. Reader boundary explaining what the score does and does not mean.

Internal rubric alone is never sufficient.

## Exposure Types

- `augmentation`: AI is likely to assist, speed up, draft, summarize, search, simulate, or check work while humans retain accountability.
- `automation`: some tasks can be executed end-to-end or near end-to-end by software under bounded conditions.
- `mixed`: both augmentation and automation are material, or task composition varies across settings.
- `unknown`: evidence is too weak for a stable classification.

## Score Bands

- `1-2`: low task exposure. Work is mostly physical, interpersonal, context-heavy, regulated, or site-specific.
- `3-4`: limited exposure. AI may assist narrow administrative, search, or documentation tasks.
- `5-6`: moderate exposure. AI can accelerate a meaningful share of analysis, documentation, communication, or routine decision support.
- `7-8`: high exposure. Many recurring cognitive, analytical, drafting, classification, coding, or information-processing tasks can be AI-assisted or partly automated, but this is not a job-loss prediction.
- `9-10`: very high exposure. Most recurring task content is information-processing or digital work with strong AI substitution/automation potential under bounded conditions; human accountability, domain judgment, regulation, and market adoption still constrain outcomes.

## Confidence

- `high`: direct occupation task evidence plus multiple external calibration sources agree.
- `medium`: direct occupation task evidence exists, but calibration sources are general or partially adjacent.
- `low`: evidence is sparse, adjacent, macro-only, or occupation settings vary widely.

## Required Reader Boundary

Reader-facing AI impact text must include a boundary equivalent to:

`This score is a task-exposure signal, not a prediction that the occupation will disappear or that an individual worker will lose income or employment.`

Localized Chinese copy must make the same boundary explicit.
