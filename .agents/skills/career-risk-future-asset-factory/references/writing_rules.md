# Career Risk Future Writing Rules

Write risk as boundaries and reality checks. Distinguish task exposure from career outcome and macro context from occupation-specific evidence.

Reader-facing copy must be localized, role-specific, concise, and explicitly bounded when evidence is partial. Do not leak evidence IDs, raw enums, source collection notes, or internal audit language.

## Locale and Market Orientation

Do not produce the English asset by translating the Chinese asset. Each locale must be independently written from the same protected evidence and score, with market context adapted to the reader.

- `zh-CN`: write for mainland China readers. Use China-specific regulatory and market boundaries only when the evidence supports them. China references such as financial regulation, C-ROSS, local recruiting signals, or domestic credential context must not be copied into the English asset as generic facts.
- `en`: write primarily for US/UK/EU English-market readers. Use O*NET, BLS, UK National Careers, European or international context, and globally recognizable professional workflows when supported by evidence. Avoid China-only regulatory references unless the occupation asset explicitly compares China as a separate boundary.
- Both locales must keep the same protected score and evidence lineage, but reader-facing examples, regulatory terms, software references, preparation advice, and market caveats may differ by locale.
- Audit must fail any row where `en` is a literal or near-literal translation of `zh-CN` and therefore carries China-only examples, Chinese terms, or local platform assumptions into the English market.

## AI Impact Copy

Use labels such as:

- `AI 任务暴露`
- `AI task exposure`
- `AI may accelerate parts of this work`

Avoid labels such as:

- `Will AI replace this career?` unless the answer immediately rejects replacement framing
- `job loss risk`
- `career disappearance risk`
- `safe from AI`
- `AI-proof`

For high scores, write that many recurring tasks may be accelerated, assisted, or partly automated. Do not write that the career is disappearing.

For low scores, write that current task evidence suggests lower exposure. Do not write that the occupation is protected or guaranteed safe.

## Domain-Specific Density

AI impact copy should use concrete occupational workflows and skill-evidence examples when evidence supports them. Prefer task-level detail such as model review, data reconciliation, scenario testing, stakeholder communication, regulatory documentation, site constraints, safety protocols, toolchains, or credential boundaries over generic phrases like `AI can improve efficiency`.

Do not add unsupported percentages, software names, regulatory labels, or market claims. High-density domain language is allowed only when it is grounded in task evidence, official profiles, or accepted source rules.
