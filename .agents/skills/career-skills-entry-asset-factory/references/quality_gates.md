# Career Skills Entry Quality Gates

Skills-entry content must help users build verifiable preparation evidence while respecting regional credential boundaries.

## PASS Requirements

- Skills, knowledge, tools, credentials, education, portfolio, internships, and hiring signals are separated.
- Credential/licensing claims have country or jurisdiction boundaries.
- Employer preference is not promoted to formal requirement.
- Each preparation item names a verifiable artifact, toolchain, project, case review, practice record, field log, or deliverable when possible.
- Local-market notes are explicit and source-bounded.
- No admission, certification, immigration, salary, hiring, promotion, or success guarantee.
- Repeated "build a portfolio / learn the basics" template copy fails unless occupationally grounded.

## Evidence Reopen Triggers

- unsourced certificate or license
- school pathway presented as universal
- toolchain claim not tied to occupation evidence
- "entry path" that is only generic study advice
- local hiring signal copied from job boards as formal rule

## Editorial QA Focus

Review medical, legal, aviation, education, skilled trades, public safety, creative, software/engineering, and low-formal-credential roles separately. Their entry evidence differs sharply.
